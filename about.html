Me
I'm not very good at self-promotion. I enjoy running, though I also like walking. I like drawing; I'm an okay graphic designer. I've always liked Computers and things that have to do with computers. I enjoy learning, and I am often finding ways to refine my learning process. Also, I keep inordinately long, meticulous to-do lists.

Memopadman?
Yeah, I dunno. Finding a proper nickname on the internet is hard. In addition to finding a handle that is appropriately descriptive and unique (forget about old stand-bys like 'spr0cket' and 'spanky'), you also have to take into consideration how easy it is to remember and type (cross out 'ratherunintelligent' and 'l3013_') and is also available on most of the services you want to use (so long, '0xBB' and 'bobdotcom').

Hence, memopadman. For the past few years, I've had the habit of recording everything I need to remember on a small spiral notepad I carry in my pocket wherever I go. This practice has significantly increased my ability to remember to do things. I've tried other methods of recording thoughts -- digital and otherwise -- but no other solutions so far have been as successful as my trusty notepads.

I suppose donning this alias is a sort of social pact to preclude investing time into further advancements in note-taking technology and resign myself to permanently pack a pen and pad in my pocket. And you know what? I'm cool with that.
